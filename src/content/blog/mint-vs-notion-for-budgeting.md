Mint was the go-to free budgeting app for over a decade — until Intuit shut it down in early 2024 and migrated users to Credit Karma. If you're a former Mint user looking for a replacement, Notion is one of the best alternatives. But how do they actually compare for budgeting? Let's break it down.

## What Happened to Mint?

Intuit discontinued Mint in March 2024, pushing users to Credit Karma's financial tools. While Credit Karma offers budget tracking, many former Mint users found it:

- More focused on credit products than budgeting
- Less customizable than the original Mint
- Heavier on financial product recommendations

This migration left millions of users looking for alternatives — and Notion has emerged as a top choice for people who want full control over their budget system.

## Why Choose Notion Over Mint/Credit Karma

### 1. Total Customization
Mint gave you preset categories and limited flexibility. Notion lets you build a budget system that matches exactly how you think about money:
- Custom categories and subcategories
- Multiple views (monthly, weekly, by category, by account)
- Linked databases for expense tracking, income, and savings goals
- Templates you can duplicate and modify

### 2. No Ads or Product Pushing
Mint/Credit Karma shows you credit card offers, loan products, and insurance quotes. Notion shows you... your budget. That's it.

### 3. Works Beyond Budgeting
Your Notion workspace can include habit tracking, meal planning, project management, and more — all connected to your financial data if you want it to be.

### 4. Data Privacy
With Mint, your transaction data was used for targeted advertising. Notion doesn't monetize your personal data.

## Why Some People Prefer Mint/Credit Karma

### 1. Automatic Bank Syncing
The biggest advantage of Mint was zero-effort transaction tracking. Notion requires manual entry (though many users find this actually improves awareness of spending).

### 2. Pre-Built Reports
Mint automatically generated spending reports, trends, and net worth tracking. In Notion, you build these yourself (or use a template).

### 3. Zero Setup Time
Credit Karma works out of the box. A Notion budget system takes 30–60 minutes to set up properly (or 5 minutes with a good template).

## FAQ

**Is Notion really free for budgeting?**
Yes. Notion's free Personal plan includes unlimited pages, databases, and blocks. You don't need a paid plan for budgeting.

**Can I import my Mint data into Notion?**
Not directly, but you can export Mint/Credit Karma transactions as CSV and import them into a Notion database using tools like Notion's CSV import or a simple copy-paste into a table.

**Is manual entry in Notion actually better than auto-sync?**
Many budgeting experts say yes. Manual entry forces you to see every transaction, which increases awareness and reduces mindless spending. Studies show people who manually track spending save 10–15% more.
