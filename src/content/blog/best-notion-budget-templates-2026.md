Managing your money shouldn't feel like a second job. If you're already using Notion for notes, tasks, or projects, adding a budget tracker to your workspace just makes sense — everything in one place, no extra apps.

But with hundreds of Notion budget templates out there, which ones are actually worth using? We tested dozens and picked the 5 best options for 2026.

## What Makes a Good Budget Template?

Before diving in, here's what we looked for:

- **Simple setup** — If it takes 30 minutes to configure, most people will never use it
- **Clear categories** — You need to see where your money goes at a glance
- **Both income and expenses** — Tracking only expenses gives you half the picture
- **Mobile-friendly** — You'll want to log expenses on the go

## 2. Notion's Built-in Budget Template

Notion offers a free budget template in their template gallery. It covers the basics — expense logging with categories and dates.

What we like: It's free and officially supported. Good starting point if you've never budgeted before.

What's missing: No income tracking, limited categories, and no sample data to learn from.

**Price:** Free

## 3. Thomas Frank's Ultimate Budget System

A comprehensive budgeting system from one of the most popular Notion creators. It includes automated calculations, monthly summaries, and detailed analytics.

What we like: Extremely thorough. If you want every possible feature, this is it.

What's missing: Can be overwhelming for beginners. The setup takes time, and the complexity might discourage daily use.

**Price:** $29

## 4. Easlo's Simple Budget Tracker

A mid-range option that balances features and simplicity. Clean design with good category management.

What we like: Nice visual design and reasonable price.

What's missing: Fewer categories out of the box compared to other options.

**Price:** $9

## 5. Reddit Community Templates

The r/Notion subreddit regularly shares free budget templates. Quality varies, but you can find some gems.

What we like: Free and community-tested. Often updated based on user feedback.

What's missing: No support, inconsistent quality, and you'll need to browse through many options to find a good one.

**Price:** Free

Here's our quick recommendation:
