# Google Sheets vs Notion for Budgeting

Google Sheets and Notion are both free, both popular for budgeting, and both work on any device. But they approach budgeting from completely different angles. Google Sheets is a spreadsheet — powerful formulas, rows, and columns. Notion is a flexible workspace — databases, views, and connected pages. Which one is better for your budget?

## Quick Comparison

| Feature | Google Sheets | Notion |
|---------|--------------|--------|
| **Price** | Free | Free |
| **Formulas** | ✅ Advanced (VLOOKUP, QUERY, macros) | ⚠️ Basic (sum, if, date functions) |
| **Templates** | ✅ Many free options | ✅ Many free + premium options |
| **Collaboration** | ✅ Real-time editing | ✅ Real-time editing |
| **Mobile App** | ⚠️ Functional but clunky | ✅ Clean mobile experience |
| **Data Visualization** | ✅ Built-in charts | ⚠️ Limited (needs workarounds) |
| **Customization** | Medium (cell formatting) | High (databases, views, pages) |
| **Offline Access** | ✅ With Chrome extension | ✅ Built-in |
| **Learning Curve** | Medium-High | Medium |

## Where Google Sheets Wins

### 1. Formulas and Calculations
Google Sheets is a spreadsheet at its core. You get:
- **SUMIF, COUNTIF, AVERAGEIF** — Calculate spending by category automatically
- **QUERY function** — Pull and filter data like a database
- **Conditional formatting** — Cells turn red when you overspend
- **IMPORTRANGE** — Pull data from other spreadsheets
- **Charts and graphs** — Visualize spending trends in seconds

For number-heavy budgeters who love formulas, nothing beats a spreadsheet.

### 2. Built-In Charts
Want a pie chart of your spending categories? Select the data, insert chart, done. Notion requires third-party integrations or manual workarounds for data visualization.

### 3. CSV Import/Export
Moving data in and out of Google Sheets is effortless. Bank exports, tax software imports, and data backups all work seamlessly with CSV.

## Where Notion Wins

### 1. Multiple Views of the Same Data
Enter expenses once, view them multiple ways:
- **Table view**: Like a spreadsheet
- **Calendar view**: See expenses by date
- **Board view**: Kanban-style by category or status
- **Gallery view**: Visual cards for each transaction

Google Sheets shows you rows and columns. That's it.

### 2. Connected Databases
In Notion, your budget database can link to:
- A savings goals tracker
- A bill payment calendar
- A subscription management page
- A financial goals page

Everything stays connected. In Google Sheets, you'd need multiple tabs and complex formulas to achieve similar linking.

### 3. Beyond Budgeting
Notion can be your budget tracker, habit tracker, meal planner, and project manager — all in one workspace. Google Sheets does spreadsheets.

### 4. Better Mobile Experience
Notion's mobile app is designed for quick data entry. Adding an expense takes 3 taps. Google Sheets on mobile is functional but navigating cells on a phone screen is frustrating.

## Real-World Comparison: Monthly Budget Tracking

### In Google Sheets
```
| Date | Category | Description | Amount | Running Total |
| 3/1  | Rent     | March rent  | $1,200 | $1,200       |
| 3/2  | Food     | Groceries   | $85    | $1,285       |
| 3/3  | Transport| Gas         | $45    | $1,330       |
```
- Add formulas to calculate totals by category
- Create a separate summary tab with SUMIF formulas
- Build a chart from the summary data

### In Notion
- Create an Expenses database with properties: Date, Category (select), Amount (number), Description (text)
- Switch to Board view to see spending by category
- Use a linked view with filters to show "this month only"
- Add a formula property for running total

Both work. The question is: do you think in cells or in databases?

| You love formulas and functions | You want multiple views of your data |
| You need advanced charts | You prefer a visual, organized workspace |
| You share budgets with an accountant | You track more than just money |
| You import/export CSV frequently | You already use Notion for other things |
| You want maximum calculation power | You want a clean mobile entry experience |

## The Hybrid Approach

Some budgeters use both:
- **Google Sheets** for the master budget with formulas, projections, and charts
- **Notion** for daily expense logging (easier on mobile) and financial planning pages

You can embed Google Sheets in Notion pages for the best of both worlds.

## FAQ

**Which is easier for beginners?**
Google Sheets if you've used Excel before. Notion if you haven't — its database approach is more intuitive than formulas for many people.

**Can Notion replace Google Sheets for budgeting?**
For most people, yes. The main things you'd miss are advanced formulas (QUERY, VLOOKUP) and built-in charts. For basic to intermediate budgeting, Notion handles everything.

**Is one more secure than the other?**
Both use encryption and are equally secure for personal budgeting. Google has the edge for enterprise security features, but for individual use, they're comparable.
