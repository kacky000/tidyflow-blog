A good budget spreadsheet can change your financial life — but finding the right one feels like searching for a needle in a haystack. There are hundreds of free templates floating around the internet, and most of them are either too basic to be useful or so complicated they take longer to set up than to actually use.

After testing dozens of budget spreadsheet templates across Excel, Google Sheets, and Notion, we've narrowed it down to the best free options for 2026. Whether you're a visual thinker who loves dashboards, a spreadsheet power user who wants full formula control, or someone who just needs a simple way to track spending, there's a template here for you.

## What Makes a Great Budget Spreadsheet Template?

Before diving into specific templates, here's what separates a genuinely useful budget spreadsheet from one you'll abandon after a week:

**Pre-built formulas that actually work.** Nothing kills budgeting momentum faster than a broken SUM formula or a cell reference error. The best templates include tested formulas for automatic calculations — totals, remaining budget, percentage spent, and month-over-month changes.

**Clear category structure.** Your template should come with sensible default categories (housing, food, transportation, entertainment) while making it easy to add your own. Rigid category structures that don't match your spending patterns lead to abandoned budgets.

**Visual summaries.** Numbers in rows and columns are useful, but charts and conditional formatting help you spot trends instantly. A dashboard view that shows your overall financial picture at a glance makes the difference between a template you check daily and one that collects dust.

**Easy monthly rollover.** The best templates let you copy your structure to a new month without starting from scratch. Even better, some include 12-month views so you can see your full year at once.

## Best Free Budget Spreadsheet Templates

### Excel Templates

#### Microsoft's Monthly Budget Template
Microsoft's own budget template is surprisingly solid for a free offering. It includes income tracking, categorized expenses with pre-built formulas, and a clean summary section with pie charts showing spending distribution. The template works in both desktop Excel and Excel Online.

**Best for:** People who already use Excel and want a no-fuss starting point. The built-in formulas handle all calculations automatically, and the layout is clean enough to use without modification.

#### Vertex42 Family Budget Planner
Vertex42 has been producing quality spreadsheet templates for over a decade, and their family budget planner is one of the most downloaded financial templates on the internet. It includes a yearly overview, monthly detail sheets, and built-in charts — all in a single workbook.

**Best for:** Families managing a household budget who want to track annual trends alongside monthly details.

#### Tiller Foundation Template
While Tiller's full service is subscription-based, their Foundation Template is free to download for Excel. What makes it stand out is the daily transaction tracking approach — instead of just setting category limits, you log individual transactions and see running totals in real time.

**Best for:** Detail-oriented budgeters who want transaction-level tracking without paying for an app.

### Google Sheets Templates

#### Google's Built-in Budget Templates
Open Google Sheets, click "Template Gallery," and you'll find annual and monthly budget templates ready to use. The monthly version includes income and expense sections with automatic totals, while the annual version gives you a 12-month overview with summary charts.

**Best for:** Google Workspace users who want the simplest possible starting point with automatic cloud syncing.

#### Tiller for Google Sheets
Tiller's Google Sheets integration is its strongest product. The free trial includes automatic bank transaction imports, categorization, and a dashboard that updates as transactions flow in. After the trial, you can keep the template structure and manually update it.

**Best for:** People who want automated bank feeds and are willing to try a 30-day trial to see if the automation is worth the subscription.

#### Reddit's r/personalfinance Community Templates
The personal finance subreddit has produced several excellent budget templates created by actual users who've refined them based on real-world feedback. These tend to be more practical and less polished than corporate offerings, but they often include clever features like debt snowball calculators and savings goal trackers.

**Best for:** Budget enthusiasts who appreciate community-tested solutions and don't mind a less polished look.

### Notion Templates

#### Notion's Official Budget Planning Template
Notion's free budget planning template takes a database approach to budgeting. Instead of rows and columns, you create entries for income and expenses that can be filtered, sorted, and viewed in multiple ways — table, calendar, or gallery. This makes it easy to see spending patterns from different angles.

**Best for:** People who want a more visual, flexible budgeting system and already use Notion for other aspects of their life. Read our detailed comparison in [Notion vs Excel for Budgeting](/notion-vs-excel-budgeting/).

#### All-in-One Finance Dashboard Templates
Several Notion creators offer free or low-cost finance dashboard templates that go beyond basic budgeting. These typically include expense tracking, savings goals, subscription management, investment tracking, and net worth calculation — all linked together in a single workspace. If you're curious about setting one up, check out our [personal finance dashboard guide](/personal-finance-dashboard-guide/).

**Best for:** People who want a complete financial management system, not just a budget tracker. Our guide on [tracking expenses in Notion](/how-to-track-expenses-in-notion/) walks you through the setup process step by step.

## How to Choose the Right Template

**If you're a complete beginner:** Start with Google Sheets' built-in monthly budget template or Microsoft's monthly budget. They're simple, free, and require zero setup beyond entering your numbers. Don't overthink it — the best budget is the one you'll actually use. If you're not sure where to start, our [monthly budget checklist](/monthly-budget-checklist/) gives you a step-by-step process.

**If you've been budgeting and want more control:** Move to Vertex42's family planner or Tiller's Foundation Template. These offer more detailed tracking and year-over-year comparisons that help you spot long-term trends.

**If you want everything in one place:** Notion's database approach lets you combine budgeting with goal tracking, bill management, and financial planning in a single workspace. It's a different way of thinking about budgeting, but many people find it more intuitive once they get past the initial setup.

## Tips for Getting the Most Out of Any Budget Template

**Customize categories to match your actual life.** Generic categories like "miscellaneous" are where budgets go to die. If you spend money on it regularly, give it its own category. Coffee shops, pet expenses, hobby supplies — being specific helps you make real decisions about your spending.

**Set it up during a calm moment.** Don't try to build your budget when bills are due or you're stressed about money. Pick a quiet Sunday afternoon, gather your last three months of bank statements, and take your time setting everything up correctly.

**Schedule a weekly check-in.** Block 10 minutes every Sunday to update your budget. This prevents the end-of-month scramble where you're trying to categorize 100+ transactions from memory. Consistency beats perfection.

**Don't try to be perfect in month one.** Your first budget will be wrong — and that's completely fine. The goal isn't perfection, it's awareness. By month three, your estimates will be surprisingly accurate because you'll have real data to work from.

**Share it with your accountability partner.** Whether that's a spouse, friend, or family member, having someone who can see your budget (even just the summary) dramatically increases follow-through. Google Sheets and Notion both make sharing easy.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best free budget spreadsheet?
The best free budget spreadsheet depends on your needs. For simplicity, Google Sheets' built-in monthly budget template works great. For detailed tracking, Vertex42's family budget planner for Excel offers the most features. For a modern, visual approach, Notion's budget planning template provides unique flexibility with database views and filters.

### Can I use a budget spreadsheet on my phone?
Yes. Google Sheets works well on both iPhone and Android through the free app. Excel also has a mobile app, though some features are limited on smaller screens. Notion offers the best mobile budgeting experience because its card-based database views are designed for mobile use.

### How often should I update my budget spreadsheet?
Update your transactions weekly at minimum — ideally every few days. Monthly check-ins for reviewing totals and adjusting next month's budget are essential. The more frequently you update, the more useful your budget becomes, and the less time each update takes.

### Is Excel or Google Sheets better for budgeting?
Google Sheets is better for collaboration and accessibility (it's free and works anywhere), while Excel offers more powerful formulas and better offline support. If you budget alone and prefer desktop apps, Excel might be your pick. If you budget with a partner or want cloud access from any device, Google Sheets wins.
